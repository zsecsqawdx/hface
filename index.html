What Are Food Additives?

Food additives are substances added to food to improve flavor, appearance, preservation, nutrition, or processing quality.

They include categories such as colorants, preservatives, antioxidants, acidity regulators, flavor enhancers, and more.

This site provides simple, science-based explanations for the most common additives.

Common Food Additives

Learn about widely used additives, their functions, and safety information.

Curcumin (E100)

Category: Colorant

Uses: Provides yellow–orange coloring in beverages, sauces, and snacks.

Safety: Considered safe at regulated levels.

Carotenoids (E160)

Category: Colorant

Uses: Widely used to give red/yellow hues.

Safety: Naturally occurring and generally safe.

Potassium Sorbate (E202)

Category: Preservative

Uses: Prevents mold and yeast growth.

Safety: Approved globally when used below limits.

Sodium Benzoate (E211)

Category: Preservative

Uses: Used in acidic beverages and sauces.

Safety: Safe within limits; avoid combining with high vitamin C.

Citric Acid (E330)

Category: Acidulant / Antioxidant

Uses: Adjusts acidity, enhances taste.

Safety: Very widely used and safe.

Monosodium Glutamate (E621)

Category: Flavor Enhancer

Uses: Boosts umami flavor.

Safety: Considered safe; some people may be sensitive at high intake.

Safety & Regulation

Food additives are evaluated by authorities such as FDA, EFSA, and WHO. Each additive has limits set based on scientific assessments, including ADI (Acceptable Daily Intake).

Labels are required to clearly show additives used in a product.
